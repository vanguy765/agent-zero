### Step 1: Shut Off Water Supply
1. Close both hot and cold shut-off valves under the sink
2. Open the faucet to relieve pressure and drain residual water
3. If shut-off valves do not hold, shut off at the main and note valve replacement is required
4. Place bucket under connections

### Step 2: Disconnect Supply Lines
1. Disconnect hot and cold supply lines from shut-off valves using adjustable wrench
2. Disconnect supply lines from faucet using basin wrench
3. Allow lines to drain into bucket
4. Cap or plug open valve outlets if sink will be out for extended period

### Step 3: Disconnect Drain Assembly
1. Loosen slip-joint nut connecting tailpiece to P-trap (channel-lock pliers)
2. Loosen slip-joint nut connecting P-trap to wall stub-out or floor drain
3. Remove P-trap assembly — dump residual water into bucket
4. Stuff rag into open drain pipe to prevent sewer gas entry
5. If garbage disposal is present: disconnect electrical, disconnect dishwasher drain hose, remove disposal from sink flange

### Step 4: Disconnect Ancillary Connections
- Dishwasher drain hose (if connected to tailpiece or disposal)
- Filtered water dispenser lines
- Soap dispenser mounting
- Instant hot water dispenser

### Step 5: Remove Faucet (if not transferring to new sink)
1. Remove faucet mounting nuts from underside using basin wrench
2. Lift faucet assembly out from above
3. Clean mounting surface

### Step 6: Remove Sink

**For undermount sinks:**
1. Cut silicone sealant between sink rim and countertop using utility knife
2. Remove mounting clips from underside
3. Support sink from below while removing last clips
4. Lower sink carefully — **two-person lift for large/heavy sinks**

**For drop-in (top-mount) sinks:**
1. Cut caulk/sealant around sink rim using utility knife
2. Remove any mounting clips from underside
3. Lift sink straight up and out

**For wall-mount sinks:**
1. Disconnect all plumbing connections
2. Remove mounting bolts/screws from wall bracket
3. Lift sink off bracket — **support sink weight throughout**

**For pedestal sinks:**
1. Disconnect all plumbing connections
2. Remove sink from wall mounting
3. Remove pedestal base

### Step 7: Inspect & Prepare
1. Inspect countertop cutout for damage, rot, or mold
2. Inspect cabinet interior for water damage
3. Clean all old putty, caulk, and adhesive from countertop surface
4. Inspect and clean drain pipe — remove rag
5. **Document and photograph** any discovered damage
6. **Report issues to supervisor / customer** before proceeding

## 9. Procedure — Installation of New Sink

### 📋 Installation Phase Groups (Executive Checklist)

Complete each group in order. Check off the group when **all tasks within it are finished**.

| Priority | Group | Tasks | ✓ |
|:---:|---|---|:---:|
| **1** | **Dry-Fit & Pre-Mount Assembly** | Verify sink fit, install faucet and drain fittings on bench before mounting | ☐ |
| **2** | **Mount & Seal Sink** | Set sink into position, install clips, apply sealant, verify level | ☐ |
| **3** | **Connect Drain System** | Install tailpiece, P-trap, trap arm; verify slope and code compliance | ☐ |
| **4** | **Connect Water Supply** | Attach supply lines to shut-off valves, replace valves if needed, verify hot/cold orientation | ☐ |
| **5** | **Reconnect Ancillaries & Prep for Test** | Reattach dishwasher, disposal, dispensers; final visual check | ☐ |

> ✅ **All 5 groups complete?** → Proceed to Section 10 — Testing & Commissioning.

---

### Step 1: Dry-Fit the New Sink
1. Place new sink into cutout (drop-in) or hold in position (undermount) to verify fit
2. Check alignment with drain and supply rough-ins
3. Verify faucet holes align with selected faucet
4. Mark position if needed with painter's tape

### Step 2: Install Faucet on New Sink (Before Mounting)
> **Best practice:** Install faucet, strainer, and drain fittings on the sink before mounting — much easier access.

1. Insert faucet through mounting holes from above
2. Install rubber gasket and mounting hardware from below
3. Hand-tighten, then snug with basin wrench — **do not overtighten**
4. Connect supply line stubs to faucet inlets (hand-tight + 1/4 turn)

### Step 3: Install Basket Strainer / Drain Fitting

**Kitchen sink (basket strainer):**
1. Roll plumber's putty into 1/2" rope
2. Apply putty ring around strainer flange
3. Insert strainer into drain hole from above
4. From below: install rubber gasket → cardboard friction ring → locknut
5. Tighten locknut while holding strainer from above to prevent rotation
6. Trim excess putty from above with putty knife

**Bathroom sink (pop-up drain):**
1. Apply plumber's putty or silicone (per manufacturer) to drain flange
2. Insert drain body from above
3. Thread drain body into tailpiece from below
4. Install pop-up linkage and adjust

### Step 4: Mount the Sink

**Drop-in (top-mount):**
1. Apply continuous bead of silicone sealant around countertop cutout edge
2. Carefully lower sink into cutout — align with marks
3. Press down firmly and evenly to seat
4. Install mounting clips from below — tighten evenly in star pattern
5. Wipe excess sealant from above and below

**Undermount:**
1. Apply silicone sealant to sink rim or countertop underside per manufacturer
2. Lift sink into position from below — **two-person operation**
3. Install mounting clips/brackets — tighten evenly
4. Support sink with temporary bracing until sealant cures (if required)
5. Clean excess sealant

**Wall-mount:**
1. Install wall bracket at correct height (31" – 34" AFF standard, or ADA height 34" max)
2. Verify bracket is level and secured to blocking/studs
3. Hang sink on bracket
4. Secure with mounting bolts

### Step 5: Connect Drain Assembly
1. Install tailpiece to strainer/drain body — hand-tight slip joint
2. Assemble P-trap:
   - Trap arm into wall stub-out (ensure proper slope — 1/4" per foot minimum)
   - P-trap bend connecting tailpiece to trap arm
3. Tighten all slip-joint nuts — **hand-tight plus 1/4 turn with pliers**
4. Ensure trap weir is not below the crown weir of the trap arm
5. Verify horizontal distance from trap weir to vent does not exceed code maximums

> ⚠️ **Code Check:** P-trap must be accessible, must have water seal of 2" – 4", and must not be double-trapped.

### Step 6: Connect Supply Lines
1. Connect braided stainless supply lines to shut-off valves
   - Hand-tight + 1/4 turn with wrench
   - **Do not use Teflon tape on compression fittings**
2. If replacing shut-off valves:
   - Cut supply pipe cleanly with tubing cutter
   - Deburr inside and outside
   - Install new 1/4-turn ball valve (compression or push-fit)
3. Verify hot is on left, cold is on right (facing the sink)

### Step 7: Reconnect Ancillary Items
- Dishwasher drain hose to disposal or tailpiece air gap
- Filtered water line
- Soap dispenser
- Instant hot water dispenser
- Garbage disposal (if included in scope)
