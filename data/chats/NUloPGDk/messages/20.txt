## 1. Purpose

This SOP establishes the standard procedure for preparing, priming, and painting interior wall surfaces with a 2-coat latex finish system. It ensures consistent surface preparation, coverage, and final appearance across all residential and light commercial interior painting jobs.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP covers:
- Interior wall surfaces: drywall, plaster, and previously painted surfaces
- Minor surface repairs (holes ≤ 3" diameter, hairline cracks)
- Application of primer coat on new, repaired, or stained surfaces
- Application of 2-coat interior latex finish

This SOP does **not** cover:
- Ceiling painting — treated as a separate scope; consult Operations Manager for procedure
- Trim and baseboard painting (see PNT-SOP-004: Trim & Baseboard Painting)
- Cabinet repainting (see PNT-SOP-003: Cabinet Repainting)
- Exterior wall or siding painting (see PNT-SOP-002: Exterior Siding Painting)
- Drywall repairs larger than 3" diameter (see DRW-SOP-001: Drywall Patch Repair)
- Active moisture, mold, or water-damage remediation (see DRW-SOP-004: Water Damage Repair)

## 7. Pre-Work Assessment

### 7.1 Site Assessment

1. Walk all walls and inspect for holes, cracks, popped nails, stains, mold, efflorescence, or peeling paint.
2. Identify any active moisture source or mold — **stop and remediate before painting**. See DRW-SOP-004.
3. If structure was built before 1978, test surfaces to be sanded for lead paint before proceeding.
4. Check adhesion of existing paint — press tape firmly and pull sharply. If existing paint lifts, the surface requires additional prep (scraping, sanding, or primer).
5. Assess existing sheen: painting flat over semi-gloss without scuff-sanding will reduce adhesion.
6. Calculate paint quantity: approx. 350–400 sq ft per gallon per coat. Order 10% extra for waste and touch-ups.
7. Confirm color, sheen, and finish with customer before purchasing materials.

### 7.2 Customer Communication

- Confirm paint color, sheen (flat, eggshell, satin), and brand with customer — obtain written or text confirmation.
- Clarify furniture relocation: who is responsible for moving heavy items before work begins.
- Advise customer of dry time: typically touch-dry in 1–2 hrs, recoat in 4 hrs, full cure in 24–48 hrs.
- Inform customer that the space will require ventilation for 24–48 hrs after completion.
- Note any accent walls, two-tone areas, or feature treatments requiring separate color application.

## 8. Procedure — Surface Preparation

### Executive Checklist

Complete each step in order. Check off the step when **all tasks within it are finished**.

| **1** | **Clear & Protect the Space** | Move or cover furniture; lay drop cloths; remove outlet and switch cover plates | ☐ |

| **7** | **Final Surface Wipe-Down** | Remove sanding dust with damp cloth or tack cloth; allow to fully dry | ☐ |

> ✅ **All steps complete?** → Proceed to Section 9 — Priming.

---

### Detailed Steps

#### Step 1: Clear & Protect the Space

1. Remove small furniture and decor from the room entirely where possible.
2. Push large furniture to the center of the room and cover with plastic sheeting.
3. Lay canvas or plastic drop cloths over the entire floor — overlap seams by 6" and secure edges with painter's tape so cloths cannot shift underfoot.
4. Remove all outlet covers, switch plates, and light fixture trim rings. Place hardware in a labeled bag.
5. Turn off HVAC supply to the room if possible to prevent paint mist from circulating through the system.

#### Step 2: Inspect & Clean Surfaces

1. Walk every wall in strong raking light (hold a work light parallel to the surface) to reveal low spots, bumps, and defects.
2. Mark all defects with a pencil circle.
3. Check for grease, soap film, or fingerprints — common near light switches, door frames, and kitchens.
4. Wipe greasy areas with a TSP (trisodium phosphate) substitute solution or degreaser; rinse with clean water and allow to dry.
5. Inspect for stains (water rings, smoke, crayon, marker). Stained areas require stain-blocking primer — do not attempt to cover with standard latex primer alone.

> ⚠️ **Code Check:** Do not paint over active mold. If black or green growth is found, stop work and follow remediation protocol before painting (see DRW-SOP-004).

#### Step 3: Patch Holes & Cracks

1. For nail holes and small dents (< 1/4"): apply a small dab of lightweight spackling with a putty knife; strike flush; allow to dry.
2. For holes 1/4" – 1" diameter: apply joint compound in layers no thicker than 1/4" per coat. Allow each coat to dry fully (typically 2–4 hrs) before adding next coat.
3. For holes 1" – 3": use a mesh patch or California patch technique. Apply joint compound in 2–3 coats. Allow full drying between coats.
4. For hairline cracks: widen slightly with putty knife, fill with joint compound, and tape with paper tape if crack is active.

> **Best practice:** Do not rush patching. Insufficient drying time is the leading cause of rework. If using a heat gun to accelerate drying, avoid scorching the compound.

#### Step 4: Sand & Feather Repairs

1. Once patches are fully dry (white throughout — no dark wet spots), sand with 120-grit on a sanding block.
2. Feather the edges of each patch so it blends seamlessly into the surrounding wall — no raised ridges.
3. For existing semi-gloss or gloss surfaces: lightly scuff-sand all walls with 120-grit to improve primer adhesion.
4. Final pass: smooth all patched areas with 220-grit for a paintable surface.
5. Wipe down the sanded surface lightly with a damp sponge to remove dust.

#### Step 5: Caulk Gaps at Trim

1. Load a caulk gun with 100% paintable latex caulk.
2. Cut the tip at a 45° angle — small opening (approx. 1/8") for fine lines.
3. Run a continuous bead along all gaps between the wall and trim (baseboards, door casings, window casings).
4. Tool the bead immediately with a wet finger or caulk tool, pressing it into the gap and smoothing flush with the wall surface.
5. Wipe away excess caulk with a damp cloth before it skins over.
6. Allow to dry per manufacturer instructions (typically 30–60 min) before taping.

#### Step 6: Mask Edges & Trim

1. Apply painter's tape to the top face of baseboards and door/window casing — press the tape edge firmly with a putty knife or 5-in-1 tool to ensure a sharp paint line.
2. Mask outlet and switch boxes (tape or use purchased covers).
3. If not cutting in free-hand at the ceiling, apply tape along the ceiling edge.

> **Best practice:** Do not leave painter's tape on surfaces longer than 24 hrs — it can lift existing paint on removal. Plan masking to coincide with the painting day.

#### Step 7: Final Surface Wipe-Down

1. Using a lightly dampened cloth or tack cloth, wipe down all wall surfaces to remove sanding dust.
2. Pay attention to corners, near trim, and at ceiling edges where dust collects.
3. Allow wall surfaces to dry completely before applying primer — damp surfaces cause primer adhesion failure.

## 9. Procedure — Priming

### Executive Checklist

Complete each step in order. Check off the step when **all tasks within it are finished**.

| Step | Step Name | Tasks | ✓ |
|:---:|---|---|:---:|
| **1** | **Select & Mix Primer** | Identify correct primer type for substrate; stir or shake thoroughly | ☐ |
| **2** | **Cut In Primer at Edges** | Brush primer at ceiling, corners, and trim edges (3–4" band) | ☐ |
| **3** | **Roll Primer on Field** | Roll primer onto main wall field; maintain wet edge | ☐ |
| **4** | **Dry & Inspect** | Allow full dry time; inspect for bleed-through; apply second prime coat if needed | ☐ |

> ✅ **All steps complete?** → Proceed to Section 10 — Painting.

---

### Detailed Steps

#### Step 1: Select & Mix Primer

1. Select primer based on substrate condition:
   - **New drywall:** PVA (polyvinyl acetate) drywall primer — seals paper face and reduces topcoat absorption.
   - **Repaired surfaces:** Standard latex primer over all patches, even if walls are not fully new.
   - **Water stains, smoke, or tannin bleed:** Shellac-based or oil-based stain-blocking primer — do not use latex primer for these applications.
   - **Repainting (good condition):** Primer may be skipped if existing surface is clean, dull, and in good adhesion — confirm with Lead Painter.
2. Stir primer thoroughly with a stir stick for at least 60 seconds, or use a drill mixer for large quantities.
3. Pour a working amount into a paint tray.

#### Step 2: Cut In Primer at Edges

1. Load a 2.5" angled brush — dip 1"–1.5" into primer, tap lightly (do not drag against the side of the can).
2. Starting at the ceiling/wall junction, cut in a 3–4" band of primer along the entire ceiling edge.
3. Continue cutting in at all inside corners, around window and door openings, and along the top edge of baseboards.
4. Maintain a wet edge — work continuously around the perimeter without letting cut-in sections dry before rolling.

#### Step 3: Roll Primer on Field

1. Load a 9" roller with primer — roll off excess on the tray ramp until the cover is evenly loaded but not dripping.
2. Starting at the top corner, apply primer in a large "W" or "M" pattern, then fill in without lifting the roller.
3. Roll from the ceiling edge down to the baseboard, overlapping each stroke by 50% to avoid lap marks.
4. Work in sections of approximately 4' wide — keep a wet edge between sections.
5. Back-roll lightly (without additional primer) to even out texture.

#### Step 4: Dry & Inspect

1. Allow primer to dry per manufacturer instructions — typically 1–2 hrs for latex, longer for shellac-based.
2. Inspect in raking light: check for stain bleed-through (discoloration showing through primer).
3. If stains bleed through latex primer, apply a second coat of stain-blocking shellac primer over affected areas only.
4. Inspect patched areas — if patches appear to absorb primer heavily (dull spots), apply a second prime coat.
5. Lightly sand any raised primer stipple or brush marks with 220-grit before topcoating.

## 10. Procedure — Painting

### Executive Checklist

Complete each step in order. Check off the step when **all tasks within it are finished**.

| Step | Step Name | Tasks | ✓ |
|:---:|---|---|:---:|
| **1** | **Select & Mix Paint** | Confirm color and sheen; box multiple cans; stir thoroughly | ☐ |
| **2** | **Cut In First Coat** | Brush first coat at ceiling, corners, and trim edges | ☐ |
| **3** | **Roll First Coat on Field** | Roll first coat onto wall field; maintain wet edge; full coverage | ☐ |
| **4** | **Dry & Inspect First Coat** | Allow full recoat time; inspect coverage and check for defects | ☐ |
| **5** | **Cut In Second Coat** | Brush second coat at edges; blend into rolled area before dry | ☐ |
| **6** | **Roll Second Coat on Field** | Roll second coat for uniform finish; feather final strokes | ☐ |
| **7** | **Remove Tape & Final Inspection** | Pull tape before full cure; inspect finish; touch up as needed | ☐ |

> ✅ **All steps complete?** → Proceed to Section 11 — Recommended Videos.

---

### Detailed Steps

#### Step 1: Select & Mix Paint

1. Verify paint cans match the approved color and sheen on the order — check the lid label and the colorant strip on the side.
2. **Box the paint:** If using multiple cans of the same color, combine into a single bucket and stir thoroughly. Subtle batch variations are invisible once boxed.
3. Stir each can thoroughly with a stir stick (60 seconds minimum), or use a drill mixer.
4. Pour working amount into a lined paint tray.

> **Best practice:** Stir paint every 30–45 minutes during the job — pigment settles, especially in darker colors.

#### Step 2: Cut In First Coat

1. Load a 2.5" angled brush — dip 1"–1.5" into paint, remove excess, and maintain a controlled bead.
2. Cut in along the ceiling/wall junction: hold the brush at 45° to the wall, using the tip of the bristles to create a clean line. Maintain a 3–4" band.
3. Cut in at all inside corners, around window and door openings, and along the top of baseboards.
4. Do not allow cut-in sections to dry before rolling — work in sections small enough to stay wet.

#### Step 3: Roll First Coat on Field

1. Load roller — roll off excess on tray ramp.
2. Begin at the cut-in edge while it is still wet. Roll in overlapping vertical strokes, working into the wet cut-in band to eliminate the brush/roller junction line.
3. Use a large "W" or "M" rolling pattern, then fill in without re-loading.
4. Maintain a wet edge — never roll into a section that has begun to dry (causes lap marks).
5. Work top to bottom, section by section, keeping edges wet.
6. The first coat is a foundation coat — full, even coverage is the goal; perfection of finish is the second coat.

#### Step 4: Dry & Inspect First Coat

1. Allow full recoat time per manufacturer instructions — typically 2–4 hrs for latex interior.
2. Inspect in raking light for holidays (missed spots), thin coverage, roller stipple, sags, and brush marks.
3. Lightly sand any drips, sags, or raised texture with 220-grit.
4. Wipe sanding dust before second coat.

#### Step 5: Cut In Second Coat

1. Repeat cut-in technique from Step 2.
2. The second coat requires greater precision — take time to achieve a clean line.
3. Immediately roll into the wet cut-in section (Step 6) to prevent lap marks.

#### Step 6: Roll Second Coat on Field

1. Repeat rolling technique from Step 3.
2. The second coat should achieve full opacity — if coverage is uneven, a third coat may be required in isolated areas.
3. Final roller strokes: use light, vertical "laying off" strokes from ceiling to baseboard without additional paint to eliminate visible roller texture.

> **Best practice:** Maintain room temperature between 50°F–90°F and humidity below 85% during application and drying. Extreme cold, heat, or humidity affects dry time and adhesion.

#### Step 7: Remove Tape & Final Inspection

1. Remove painter's tape **before the paint fully cures** — ideally when paint is dry to the touch but not hard (approximately 1–2 hrs after final coat).
2. Pull tape back at a 45° angle toward the painted surface (not away from it) to prevent lifting paint.
3. If the tape pulls paint, score the tape edge lightly with a utility knife before pulling.
4. Inspect the entire room in raking light: look for holidays, sags, lap marks, and tape bleed.
5. Touch up as needed with a small brush, feathering edges into the surrounding surface.

## 12. Testing & Commissioning

### Step 1: Visual Inspection

- [ ] No visible brush marks, sags, drips, or roller stipple from 5 ft viewing distance
- [ ] Uniform sheen across entire wall surface — no flat or shiny spots
- [ ] Full coverage — no holidays (missed spots) or thin areas showing substrate or previous color
- [ ] Clean paint lines at ceiling, trim, and adjacent walls — no tape bleed
- [ ] All patches invisible under the final coat

### Step 2: Color & Sheen Verification

- [ ] Color matches customer-approved sample in the room's natural and artificial lighting
- [ ] Sheen level consistent with specification (eggshell, satin, or flat)
- [ ] No color variation between sections (batch mismatch or inconsistent mixing)

### Step 3: Final Verification

- [ ] All painter's tape removed without pulling existing paint
- [ ] Outlet covers and switch plates reinstalled
- [ ] Drop cloths removed; floor free of paint drips
- [ ] No paint on trim, windows, ceiling, or flooring (or touch-up of overspray complete)

## 14. Troubleshooting

| Issue | Probable Cause | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| Paint peeling after drying | Poor surface prep; contamination; insufficient primer | Strip affected section, clean thoroughly, prime correctly, repaint |
| Lap marks / roller lines visible | Rolling into dry sections; wrong nap size; paint drying too fast | Maintain wet edge; work smaller sections; add conditioner (e.g., Floetrol) in hot/dry conditions |
| Drips and sags | Over-applied paint; coat applied too thick | Allow to dry fully; sand smooth; apply thinner coat |
| Blotchy or uneven color | Insufficient primer on porous patches; skipped prime coat | Apply PVA primer to all patched areas; repaint |
| Sheen variation between sections | Different paint batches; inconsistent technique | Box all cans before starting; apply consistent pressure and roller speed |
| Paint bleed under tape | Tape not pressed firmly; surface dirty under tape | Score tape edge carefully; remove tape; touch up with small brush |
| Brush marks at cut-in visible | Paint drying in brush between cut-in and rolling | Immediately roll into wet cut-in; use conditioner in brush paint to extend open time |
| Stains bleeding through topcoat | Inadequate stain-blocking primer | Apply shellac-based primer over stained areas; repaint |

## 15. Quality Standards

- No visible brush marks, roller lines, sags, or drips at normal viewing distance (5 ft)
- Uniform sheen across the entire wall surface — no inconsistencies
- Clean paint lines at ceiling, trim, and adjacent walls — gaps ≤ 1/16"
- Full opacity — no substrate, previous color, or patch visible
- Color confirmed to match customer-approved sample in room lighting
- All repairs fully covered and invisible under final coat
- No paint on trim, glass, flooring, or ceiling (or thoroughly cleaned)

## 5. Standard PPE

Required for all painting procedures unless the individual SOP notes exceptions or additions.

- Safety glasses or goggles — splash protection when opening cans, pouring, or rolling; required when working overhead
- Nitrile gloves — chemical resistance to paint, primer, caulk, and solvents
- N95 respirator or P100 half-mask — required when sanding any surface; VOC-rated respirator required if using solvent-based products
- Non-slip closed-toe footwear
- Protective coveralls (recommended to prevent clothing contamination)
- Knee pads — required for baseboard-level and low-surface work

The following hazards apply across all painting procedures. Individual SOPs list additional task-specific hazards.

| Hazard | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Lead paint disturbance (pre-1978 structures) | Test surfaces before any sanding or scraping; follow EPA RRP protocol; never dry-sand lead paint; use HEPA vacuum on all sanding dust |
| VOC inhalation | Open windows and exterior doors; use box fans exhausting to exterior; use low-VOC or zero-VOC products where possible |

| Skin and respiratory irritation | Wear nitrile gloves and respirator when sanding or using solvent-based products; wash skin immediately on contact |

## 7. Standard Cleanup, Documentation & Customer Handoff

### Cleanup

1. Remove all painter's tape — pull slowly at a 45° angle toward the painted surface.
2. Remove drop cloths; fold paint-side in; clean or dispose of properly.
3. Inspect floor for paint drips — clean wet paint immediately with a damp cloth; dried latex can be gently scraped with a plastic scraper.
4. Clean brushes: rinse latex paint brushes under warm water, working paint out from heel to tip; hang or lay flat to dry.
5. Reseal all paint cans tightly; label with date opened, color, sheen, and room.

### Documentation

1. Record paint color name, code, formula, sheen, and brand on the work order.
2. Photograph completed work — one photo per wall or surface area, angled in raking light to reveal finish quality.
3. Log quantity of paint used per room or area.
4. Note any conditions flagged for follow-up (e.g., monitored stain areas, cracked joints, active settlement).

### Customer Handoff

- Walk customer through the completed space; identify any areas that may benefit from a future touch-up coat.
- Provide leftover paint in a clearly labeled container for customer touch-ups.
- Advise: ventilate the space for 24–48 hrs after completion.
- Advise: allow full cure time before cleaning or placing items against painted surfaces (typically 24–48 hrs touch-safe; 7 days for full hardness).
- Obtain customer sign-off on the work order.
