# Standard Operating Procedure
## Painting — Trim & Baseboard Painting

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **SOP Number** | PNT-SOP-004 |
| **Trade** | Painting |
| **Task** | Interior trim and baseboard painting — surface prep, prime, and 2-coat brush finish |
| **Revision** | 1.0 |
| **Effective Date** | 2026-03-08 |
| **Approved By** | DRAFT |
| **Estimated Duration** | 2–4 hrs per room (varies by linear footage and condition) |

---

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines the standard procedure for preparing, priming, and painting interior trim and baseboard surfaces including door casings, window casings, baseboards, and crown molding. It ensures a clean, durable, and professional finish consistent with industry quality standards.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP covers:
- Interior baseboards, door casings, window casings, and crown molding
- Priming bare wood, MDF, or previously painted trim in poor condition
- Application of 2-coat semi-gloss or gloss brush finish on trim surfaces

This SOP does **not** cover:
- Interior wall painting (see PNT-SOP-001: Interior Wall Painting)
- Exterior trim or fascia painting (see PNT-SOP-002: Exterior Siding Painting)
- Cabinet face or door painting (see PNT-SOP-003: Cabinet Repainting)
- Full trim removal and reinstallation (see CRP-SOP-001: Interior Door Replacement)
- Wood staining or clear coat finish — this SOP covers opaque paint only

---

## 3. References & Codes

See [REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md](REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md) — Painting Trade: Codes & Standards Reference.

> ⚠️ **Code Check:** Trim and windows in pre-1978 homes are among the highest-risk surfaces for lead paint. Test before sanding. Follow EPA RRP (40 CFR Part 745) protocol strictly.

---

## 4. Personnel & Qualifications

See [REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md](REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md) — Section 4: Personnel & Qualifications.

**SOP-specific note:** Painter's Helper may prep and mask but may not sand independently on pre-1978 surfaces.

---

## 5. Safety Requirements

### 5.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

See [REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md](REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md) — Section 5: Standard PPE.

**SOP-specific note:** Knee pads are essential (not optional) for all baseboard work.

### 5.2 Hazard Awareness

See [REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md](REF_PNT_Codes_Standards.md) — Section 6: Common Hazard Awareness.

**Additional hazards for this SOP:**

| Hazard | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Prolonged kneeling (baseboard work) | Use knee pads; take breaks; alternate kneeling and squatting |

### 5.3 Pre-Work Safety Checklist

- [ ] Lead paint test completed on any trim surface to be sanded in a pre-1978 structure
- [ ] Ventilation confirmed: windows open or fan exhausting to exterior
- [ ] Knee pads available for baseboard work
- [ ] Ladder inspected — rated for user weight, non-slip feet, all locks engaged
- [ ] SDS sheets reviewed for all products and accessible on site
- [ ] Drop cloths placed under all work areas to catch drips

# Standard Operating Procedure
## Plumbing — Sink Replacement

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **SOP Number** | PLB-SOP-001 |
| **Trade** | Plumbing |
| **Task** | Remove & Replace Sink (Kitchen / Bathroom / Utility) |
| **Revision** | 1.0 |
| **Effective Date** | 2026-02-26 |
| **Approved By** | _________________________ |
| **Estimated Duration** | 2 – 4 hours (varies by scope) |

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the safe, code-compliant steps for removing an existing sink and installing a replacement sink, including all associated supply lines, drain connections, and fixtures. It applies to kitchen, bathroom, and utility sinks in residential and light-commercial settings.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP covers:
- Disconnection and removal of the existing sink, faucet, and drain assembly
- Preparation of the countertop or mounting surface
- Installation of the new sink, faucet, supply lines, and drain assembly
- Leak testing and final inspection

This SOP does **not** cover:
- Countertop fabrication or modification beyond standard sink cutout adjustments
- Relocation of supply or drain rough-in (see SOP PLB-SOP-010)
- Garbage disposal installation (see SOP PLB-SOP-005)
- Dishwasher connection (see SOP PLB-SOP-006)

---

## 3. References & Codes

- International Plumbing Code (IPC) — current edition
- Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC) — current edition (where applicable)
- Local jurisdiction amendments
- Manufacturer installation instructions for specific sink and faucet models
- OSHA 29 CFR 1926 — Construction Industry Standards

---

## 4. Personnel & Qualifications

| Role | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Lead Plumber | Licensed journeyman plumber or equivalent |
| Apprentice (if assisting) | Enrolled apprentice under direct supervision |
| Inspector (if required) | Local AHJ plumbing inspector for final sign-off |

---

## 5. Safety Requirements

### 5.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Safety glasses / goggles
- Work gloves (cut-resistant recommended)
- Knee pads (for under-cabinet work)
- Steel-toe boots
- Dust mask (if cutting or grinding)

### 5.2 Hazard Awareness
| Hazard | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Water damage | Shut off supply valves; have towels/bucket ready |
| Sharp edges | Wear cut-resistant gloves; deburr all cut pipe |
| Mold / mildew exposure | Wear N95 mask if mold is present; report to supervisor |
| Electrical proximity | Verify no live wiring near work area; use GFCI-protected circuits |
| Back strain | Use proper lifting technique; get assistance for heavy sinks (cast iron, stone) |
| Lead solder (older homes) | Test if pre-1986 plumbing; follow lead-safe work practices if positive |

### 5.3 Pre-Work Safety Checklist
- [ ] Water supply shut off and verified (both hot and cold)
- [ ] Work area cleared and protected (drop cloths, floor protection)
- [ ] Electrical circuits near sink area identified

# Standard Operating Procedure
## Electrical — Outlet Replacement

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **SOP Number** | ELC-SOP-001 |
| **Trade** | Electrical |
| **Task** | Remove & Replace Electrical Outlet (Receptacle) — 120V/240V Residential & Light-Commercial |
| **Revision** | 1.0 |
| **Effective Date** | 2026-02-28 |
| **Approved By** | _________________________ |
| **Estimated Duration** | 30 min – 2 hours (varies by scope and circuit complexity) |

---

## 1. Purpose

This procedure defines the safe, code-compliant steps for removing an existing electrical outlet (receptacle) and installing a replacement. It covers standard duplex receptacles, GFCI receptacles, USB-equipped receptacles, and 240V receptacles in residential and light-commercial settings.

---

## 2. Scope

This SOP covers:
- De-energizing the circuit and verifying zero-energy state
- Removal of the existing receptacle, cover plate, and wiring connections
- Installation of the replacement receptacle with proper wiring terminations
- Testing for correct wiring, grounding, and GFCI protection
- Cover plate installation and final commissioning

This SOP does **not** cover:
- New circuit installation or home run wiring (see ELC-SOP-005: New Branch Circuit Installation)
- Electrical panel upgrades or breaker replacement (see ELC-SOP-010: Panel & Breaker Service)
- Whole-room rewiring or knob-and-tube replacement (see ELC-SOP-015: Legacy Wiring Remediation)
- Dedicated 240V circuit installation for appliances (see ELC-SOP-020: Dedicated Appliance Circuit)
- Smart outlet / home automation integration (see ELC-SOP-025: Smart Device Installation)
- Outdoor / wet-location receptacle installation requiring new weatherproof boxes (see ELC-SOP-030: Exterior Receptacle Installation)

---

## 3. References & Codes

- National Electrical Code (NEC / NFPA 70) — current edition
- Local jurisdiction amendments and permit requirements
- Manufacturer installation instructions for specific receptacle models
- OSHA 29 CFR 1926 Subpart K — Electrical (Construction)
- OSHA 29 CFR 1910 Subpart S — Electrical (General Industry)
- NFPA 70E — Standard for Electrical Safety in the Workplace

---

## 4. Personnel & Qualifications

| Role | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Lead Electrician | Licensed journeyman electrician or equivalent per jurisdiction |
| Apprentice (if assisting) | Enrolled electrical apprentice under direct supervision |
| Inspector (if required) | Local AHJ electrical inspector for final sign-off |

---

## 5. Safety Requirements

### 5.1 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- Safety glasses / goggles
- Insulated electrical gloves (rated for voltage class — minimum Class 00 for 120V)
- Leather protector gloves over insulated gloves
- Non-conductive / dielectric footwear
- Arc-rated clothing (if panel work required — minimum 8 cal/cm²)
- Hearing protection (if using power tools)

### 5.2 Hazard Awareness
| Hazard | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Electrical shock (120V/240V) | LOTO procedure; verify zero-energy with rated tester; never trust a single test |
| Arc flash | Wear arc-rated PPE; maintain safe approach boundaries per NFPA 70E |
| Fire risk | Verify wire gauge matches breaker rating; ensure tight connections; no backstab on 20A circuits |
| Damaged / deteriorated wiring | Inspect all visible wiring; report and remediate before proceeding |
| Asbestos (older homes) | If suspected in wall material, stop work and report; do not disturb |
| Aluminum wiring (pre-1976 homes) | Identify by color (silver vs. copper); use CO/ALR rated devices only; apply anti-oxidant compound |
